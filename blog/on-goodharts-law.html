On Goodhart's Law

2026-06-26

Goodhart's Law, named after Charles Goodhart, can be stated as:

"When a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure." - Marilyn Strathern

When I was a teenager, I ran into this law. I would look for missions to serve. I'd find one, think that I had discovered what the meaning of my life would be, and after a few days I'd realise that the full realisation of whatever worldview I had addopted would not create a world/life I would want to live in.
For a while I became nihilistic, believing that there was no ideal to srive towards. Today I disagree. Despite the fact that you can't rely on any single heuristic to measure your actions, it does not mean that there is nothing to measure. Rather than giving up the search for The One Mission to Rule Them All, become an orcestra of different pursuits in appropriate contexts. In time you may find a mission that gives meaning to the ones you enjoy.

Why I want to write

2026-06-27

"By criticizing our theories we can let our theories die in our stead." - Karl Popper
Rather than having to find out what would happen if we did something by physically doing so, we can instead tell a story in our mind of what would happen. This way we can test numerous ideas before expending effort in executing one.
But for this to work you have to have a good simulator. If the story in your mind does not take Reality into account it will likely fail when you attempt to implement it in Reality.

Words matter. If the substrate of your simulator are ideas, and the substrate of your ideas are words, then words mater. When telling a story, the exact choice of words used to tell it defines the story's reality - it's physics.

I want to write because I want to close the simulation-to-reality gap between the stories in my head, and Reality as much as possible. I want to have an accurate map. If my thoughts are forever in my head, forever vague, forever safe from criticism, how will I know that they are any good? By putting my ideas into words, others can load them into their simulators, and inform me if my story leads to catastrophy.

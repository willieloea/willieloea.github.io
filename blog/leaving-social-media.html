Leaving Social Media

2026-04-24

Around 6 April 2026, I deleted my x.com account and configured my home router to not allow my phone access to youtube.com. I did this because I frequently yearn to be someone I'm not, and my engagement with these two sites took away time I should have spent becoming who I want to be.

I attempted to use these sites in a way that was healthy for me. But unhealthy patterns of behaviour would always emerge. On YouTube, I turned off my watch history so that videos weren't recommended on the home page and the recommended videos to the side of a video aren't very relevant to me. But I would always think of some YouTuber that may have released a video I would find interesting, or I would spend hours watching generic things like movie clips or car videos. On X, I used feedback mechanisms like the 'like' button or saying "Not interested in this post" and could curate my feed to some extent (this worked quite well for only seeing posts that were just text). But I would eventually regress to conversations I did not want to know about - it only takes a few lazy engagements to corrupt your feed. There were many other attempts made, but the point is that they failed over time.

What are the incentives of platforms with ad-driven-revenue? What are the chances that those are inline with your goals? I found my engagement with these platforms to be toxic towards my growth, and so I decided to leave.
